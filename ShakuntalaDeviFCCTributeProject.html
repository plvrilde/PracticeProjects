Shakuntala Devi

The Human-Calculator who Surpassed the Human-Made Computers

Life

  • Birth: 4 Novemeber 1929 in Bangalore, Karnataka (Previously Kingdom of Mysore)
  • Worked with father at road shows by displaying abilities of calculation.
  • Moved to London, UK in 1944.
  • Travelled the world demonstrating her mental calculation abilities.
  • Married in 1964. Divorced in 1979.
  • Ran for election against Indira Gandhi in 1980.
  • Spent the rest of her life in activism, education, and writing.
  • Died in 2013 at the age of 83.

Mental Ability

  • 1977 at Southern Methodist University, 23rd root of a 201 digit number within 50 seconds.
  • 18 June 1980, Multiplication of two random 13-digit numbers.
  • 1988 at University of California-Berkley, Cube root of 95443993 (in 2 sec), 204336469 (in 5 sec) & 2373927704 (in 10 sec).
  • Able to find out day and date using some simple data.
  • Long Multiplications for which special programs had to be written.
  • Calculation of Higher Roots.
  • And so Many More.........

Activism

  • Established Shakuntala Devi Education Foundation Public Trust to provide quality education to children from underprivileged backgrounds.
  • Spread Global Awareness about India's contribution towards Mathematics.
  • Wrote a book and conducted first comprehensive study on homosexuality in India

Accomplishments

  • 1969, Most Distinguished Women of the Year, by University of Philippines.
  • 1982, Fastest Human Computation, Guiness World Record
  • 1988, Received Ramanujan Mathematical Genius Award in Washington, D.C.
